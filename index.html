AI Creativity Systems

An analysis of modern text-to-image and text-to-video platforms, their architecture, capabilities, and limitations.

Research Newsletter | 2026

Overview

Artificial intelligence creative systems have transitioned from basic text generators into multimodal platforms capable of synthesizing high-resolution imagery, dynamic video, and audio outputs from natural language prompts. These systems rely primarily on transformer-based architectures for language understanding and diffusion models for media generation.

The objective of this research is to examine how these systems function, how major platforms differ in structure and accessibility, and what implications these differences create for users and creators.

Technical Foundation

Diffusion models generate images or video by starting with random noise and progressively refining that noise into structured output. Each step removes uncertainty based on learned statistical relationships between textual descriptions and visual features.

Transformer architectures interpret the prompt itself. They analyze contextual word relationships, allowing the system to understand style, composition, realism, and environmental context. In video systems, temporal modeling ensures frame-to-frame consistency.
