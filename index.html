AI RESEARCH NEWSLETTER

Phase 2: Structured Tool Analysis & Comparison

Introduction

This research evaluates the evolution of AI image generation, specifically comparing Google's Nano Banana 2 and OpenAI's DALL-E 3. The goal of this newsletter is to demonstrate how prompt engineering—specifically technical descriptive layering—affects the output of different AI architectures. We are testing for consistency, lighting accuracy, and instruction adherence.

AI PROMPT TEST

Test 01: The Baseline
"A futuristic city"
Google: Nano Banana 2
OpenAI: DALL-E 3

Evaluation:

Both models produced a wide-angle city view. Google's model leaned toward realistic materials, while DALL-E 3 provided a more vibrant, high-saturation digital look.

Test 02: Structural Detail
"A futuristic city at night with neon lights, flying cars, and tall glass skyscrapers"
Google: Nano Banana 2
OpenAI: DALL-E 3

Evaluation:

The addition of "night" and "neon" improved the contrast. Nano Banana 2 handled the light bloom from the neon signs more naturally than the DALL-E 3 counterpart.

Test 03: Professional Fidelity
"A cyberpunk futuristic city at night, neon pink and blue lighting, flying cars in the sky, holographic advertisements, rainy streets reflecting light, ultra-detailed, cinematic lighting, 4K, wide angle."
Google: Nano Banana 2
OpenAI: DALL-E 3

Evaluation:

This final test shows that Google’s Nano Banana 2 excels at environmental reflections (the rainy streets), whereas DALL-E 3 provides better clarity for the holographic advertisements.
