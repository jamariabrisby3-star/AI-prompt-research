Phase 2 | Volume 1: Cross-Model Image Generation Benchmarking

Introduction

Welcome to this specialized edition of the AI Research Newsletter. This study establishes a structured tool analysis methodology by benchmarking Google Nano Banana 2 against OpenAI DALL-E 3.

Our research focuses on descriptive layering—analyzing how technical modifiers such as lighting vectors and environmental textures are interpreted across different neural architectures. This study evaluates each engine’s latent consistency and its capability to synthesize photorealistic details while maintaining structural grounding.

COMPARATIVE EVALUATION: GOOGLE vs. OPENAI

Evaluation Case 01: Baseline Structural Fidelity
"A futuristic city"
Google: Nano Banana 2
OpenAI: DALL-E 3

Technical Analysis:

Google’s Nano Banana 2 prioritized architectural logic and realism in its initial interpretation, while DALL-E 3 leaned toward a vibrantly saturated digital-art aesthetic. Google established the realistic "grounding" that remained stable throughout the session.

Evaluation Case 02: Environmental Detail & Illumination
"A futuristic city at night with neon lights..."
Google: Nano Banana 2
OpenAI: DALL-E 3

Technical Analysis:

The addition of nighttime lighting triggered the "Reflective Engine" in Nano Banana 2, providing natural light bloom. Notably, Google's model maintained the same building structure from Test 1, whereas DALL-E 3 generated an entirely new scene, showing a lack of latent consistency.

Evaluation Case 03: Professional Fidelity Stress Test
"A cyberpunk futuristic city at night... rainy streets... 4K, wide angle."
Google: Nano Banana 2
OpenAI: DALL-E 3

Technical Analysis:

In this final test, Nano Banana 2 accurately simulated the complex ray-tracing of rainy reflections and obeyed the "wide angle" tag. DALL-E 3 provided better holographic clarity but failed to maintain environmental depth, prioritizing an illustrative look over cinematic realism.
